Toxicity & Precautions

"Natural" does not mean "harmless". The use of medicinal plants requires strict discipline and knowledge.

The dose makes the poison

The greatest danger in herbal medicine is an overdose. A handful of leaves does not always have the same concentration of active ingredients depending on the season or soil. Golden Rule: Never exceed the doses recommended by experienced traditional practitioners (often expressed in a precise number of leaves, e.g., 3 leaves per cup, no more).

Vulnerable Populations

  • Pregnant women: Many plants (such as Bitter Melon/Asosi) can induce abortions and cause miscarriages.
  • Young children: Their livers and kidneys are not mature enough to filter out certain plant toxins. Never administer herbal teas without medical advice.

Drug Interactions

Mixing herbal teas with pharmaceutical drugs can be fatal. For example, taking a plant that acts as a blood thinner at the same time as heart medication can cause severe bleeding. Always inform your doctor about the plants you are consuming.
